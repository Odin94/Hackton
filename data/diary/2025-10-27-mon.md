Boolean algebra today. AND, OR, NOT, XOR. The logic gate layer that sits underneath everything digital. The connection to set operations is explicit: De Morgan's laws are the same in both domains — complement distributes over union/intersection the same way NOT distributes over OR/AND.

The professor introduced normal forms — Disjunctive Normal Form and Conjunctive Normal Form — which are ways of expressing any boolean function as a canonical expression. DNF is the OR of AND-clauses, CNF is the AND of OR-clauses.

The algorithm for DNF from a truth table is mechanical: collect every row where the output is 1, write a minterm (AND of all variables, negated if 0 in that row), OR them together. I practiced this in my notes during the lecture. Got it.
