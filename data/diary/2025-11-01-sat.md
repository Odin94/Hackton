Analysis: Taylor series. The idea that you can approximate any smooth function with polynomials — and that the polynomial is built from the function's derivatives at a single point — is genuinely beautiful to me. The fact that e^x equals its own Taylor series everywhere, that sin(x) alternates between its odd-degree terms, that ln(1+x) has this specific radius of convergence — there's a structural elegance here that feels more like pure maths than anything else in the course.

I spent three hours on this and didn't feel like I was studying.

The Lagrange remainder term is harder. It quantifies how wrong your approximation is when you stop at n terms. The formula involves the (n+1)th derivative evaluated at some unknown point c between 0 and x. The "unknown c" part is unsatisfying — you can bound the error but you can't pinpoint it.
