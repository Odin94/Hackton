**After Informatik lecture**

OOP fundamentals. I went in expecting to coast and it was mostly fine, but the formal treatment of abstract classes vs interfaces tripped me up a bit. In Python the distinction is blurry — you can simulate both with ABCs and duck typing. Java forces the distinction at the language level, which is more rigid but also more honest about what you're expressing.

TumTum pinged after and asked what was new vs familiar. I gave it an honest breakdown: inheritance and polymorphism familiar, static typing and the Java type hierarchy less intuitive than expected, checked exceptions completely unfamiliar. It said checked exceptions would come up in the Informatik exercise sessions and not to worry about them yet.
