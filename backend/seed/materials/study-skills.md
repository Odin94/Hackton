# Study Skills — Pomodoro and Spaced Repetition

Two evidence-based techniques that compound well together.

## Pomodoro technique

Work in focused intervals of 25 minutes, followed by a 5-minute break. After four
such intervals, take a longer break of 15-30 minutes. The fixed length of the
interval makes procrastination harder: "just 25 minutes" is a smaller commitment
than "study until done". The forced breaks prevent burnout and give the mind time
to consolidate.

Variations: 50/10 for deeper work, 90/20 for writing or coding sessions. Pick the
length that matches the kind of focus the task requires.

## Spaced repetition

Review material at increasing intervals: 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days, 30 days.
Each successful recall strengthens memory more than re-reading. The technique
exploits the spacing effect: distributed practice produces better long-term
retention than massed practice (cramming).

Tools like Anki automate the scheduling. The Leitner system is a paper-based
alternative using flashcards and five boxes.

## Combining them

A productive session: pomodoro-paced review of spaced-repetition flashcards,
interleaved with new material. The pomodoro structure forces engagement; the
spaced repetition ensures what you learn sticks.

## When these fail

Pomodoros can become busywork if the tasks themselves are unclear. Always start a
session by writing down what success looks like for that session. Spaced
repetition needs initial encoding to be good — if you don't understand the
material the first time, repetition alone won't rescue it.
